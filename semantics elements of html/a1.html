Facts about Cats

Cats are believed to be the only mammals who don't taste sweetness. Cats are nearsighted, but their peripheral vision and night vision are much better than that of humans. Cats are supposed to have 18 toes (five toes on each front paw; four toes on each back paw). Cats can jump up to six times their length.Cats conserve energy by sleeping for an average of 13 to14 hours a day. Cats have flexible bodies and teeth adapted for hunting small animals such as mice and rats. A group of cats is called a clowder, a male cat is called a tom, a female cat is called a molly or queen while young cats are called kittens.

General ways to take care of cats

First, feed your cat food that is high in protein and low in carbohydrates. This will help keep them healthy and active. Second, give them fresh water and occasional litter changes. The food you choose should be balanced for the life stage of your cat or kitten. Properly balanced foods will contain taurine.
